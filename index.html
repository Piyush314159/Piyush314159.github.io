About Me

I am an MSc Physics student at IIT Hyderabad with a strong focus on data analysis, computational modeling, and machine learning applied to modern physics experiments.

My work lies at the intersection of physics and computation, where I develop and apply numerical simulations, statistical inference, and machine learning models to extract physical insight from complex, high-dimensional, and noisy datasets.

Recently, my interests have expanded strongly toward high-energy physics (HEP), particularly the use of graph neural networks and data-driven reconstruction techniques in collider experiments. Through exposure to contemporary HEP literature and ongoing project work, I am developing familiarity with detector-level data, event reconstruction pipelines, and ML-based inference methods used in experiments such as Belle II.
